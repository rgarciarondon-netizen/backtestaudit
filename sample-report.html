Findings

F1 — Strategy B has no edge even before costs CONFIRMED

Gross: +3.1% (p=0.41), −4.9% (p=0.51), −15.5% (p=0.63) across the three symbols, 176–359 trades each. Net: −45% to −56%, PF 0.70–0.76. Noise test: 0–0.7% of 150 noise-perturbed series stay profitable — the signal is the noise. Vs-random: percentiles 30–43 (worse than the median of random entries). Deflated Sharpe ≈ 0. Nothing here survives to be optimized: buying oversold on a fat-tailed asset is conceptually broken, not mistuned.

F2 — Strategy A on SOL: the "edge" that was beta CONFIRMED

The teaching case. At first glance it looks legitimate: +547.7% gross, p=0.025 (significant at 5%), +57.9% NET, PF 1.13, noise test 72.7% profitable. Four tests dismantle it:

  • Vs-random percentile 48: it does NOT beat 500 strategies of random entries with matched frequency and holding time. Any long exposure to SOL made money — SOL returned +2,596% in the window. The strategy harvests the asset's drift, not a signal.
  • Deflated Sharpe 0.61 (<0.95): adjusted for the SL/TP design trials, significance evaporates.
  • MinTRL: needs 974 trades, has 587 — sample insufficient even ignoring the above.
  • Max drawdown −87%, and buy & hold beats it 45×. Nobody survives that drawdown to collect the +58%.

A p-value alone (0.025) would have approved this strategy. The vs-random test is what exposes beta dressed as alpha. This is why the battery has more than one blade.

F3 — Costs eat all of Strategy A's gross CONFIRMED

BTC: +153% gross → −45% net (0.24% round trip × 639 trades). ETH: +274% → −9.7%. The per-trade gross edge is smaller than the friction. Net MinTRL on BTC: 6,692,603 trades — the absurd number is the formula working correctly: with net SR ≈ 0, no finite sample will ever validate it.

F4 — The client's live track record is non-informative CONFIRMED

~40 live closes accumulated. At any plausible Sharpe, MinTRL demands hundreds to thousands. Even if the live PnL were positive, 40 trades cannot establish anything — live data of this size validates infrastructure, not signal.

F5 — What survives the audit NOT REFUTED

The client's risk/execution layer (drawdown and losing-streak breakers, per-symbol cooldowns, position caps, boot-time reconciliation, feed self-healing) is outside the scope of this statistical battery and has real event-based validations on record. That layer is the asset.

Declared assumptions (attack them)

One position per symbol (serializes signals; the live system's caps/cooldowns further reduce exposure → this port is generous). No streak-based risk cuts or breakers (also generous). Costs: 0.10% taker + 0.02% slippage per side. Intrabar SL/TP tie counts as a loss. Multi-timeframe confirmation mapped to the last closed bar. Signal on close → entry at next open. Long-only spot.
